# Key Findings — Ecommerce Funnel Analysis
**Dataset:** REES46 Multi-Category Store, October 2019  
**Author:** Prasanna Pingale

---

## Finding 1 — Electronics is carrying the whole store
**Metric:** 3.18% view-to-purchase, 56% of all traffic  
**So what:** The site average conversion rate flatters every other category. 
Electronics is the only category performing at a healthy level. All 
conversion targets should be benchmarked against electronics, not the 
blended average.  
**Recommendation:** Set category-specific conversion targets. Use electronics 
as the ceiling, not the floor.

---

## Finding 2 — Apparel has a broken funnel
**Metric:** 14,362 views, 0.48% conversion, near-zero cart rate  
**So what:** Users land on apparel product pages and leave without engaging. 
This is not a traffic problem — it is a product page problem. The most 
likely causes are missing size/fit information and weak trust signals.  
**Recommendation:** Run session recordings on apparel product pages. 
Prioritise this above everything else — it has the most obvious room 
to improve and the largest gap between traffic and revenue.

---

## Finding 3 — The store has a confident mid-to-premium buyer base
**Metric:** Conversion below 1.6% under $100, above 2% from $100 to $2k, 
collapses to 0.88% above $2k  
**So what:** Price is not the barrier for this audience below $2k. Budget 
products underperform despite being the most accessible — a trust or 
quality perception problem, not a price sensitivity problem.  
**Recommendation:** Invest in trust signals (reviews, ratings, return 
policies) for sub-$100 products. Do not discount — it will not move 
conversion for this audience.

---

## Finding 4 — Brand recognition converts better than price
**Metric:** Apple 4.06% at avg $868, Samsung 4.30% at avg $334  
**So what:** Users who shop by brand arrive ready to buy. The store does 
not need to convince them — it just needs to surface them efficiently. 
Smaller brands sit at 1-2% regardless of price.  
**Recommendation:** Increase brand filter prominence and search placement 
for Samsung, Apple, Xiaomi and Huawei. These brands self-convert.

---

## Finding 5 — Nobody comes back to finish their purchase
**Metric:** 94.4% of purchases within 1 hour, median 2.4 minutes  
**So what:** This is an impulse store. Retargeting campaigns, abandoned 
cart emails and re-engagement flows are almost certainly wasted budget. 
The decision happens in session or not at all.  
**Recommendation:** Redirect retargeting budget into in-session friction 
removal — faster load times, guest checkout, fewer confirmation steps.

---

## Finding 6 — Repeat buyers are a small but enormously valuable segment
**Metric:** 19% of buyers, $926 avg spend vs $305 for one-time buyers  
**So what:** Repeat buyers are worth 3x one-time buyers but make up less 
than a fifth of the customer base. There is no evidence the store is 
doing anything specific to nurture them.  
**Recommendation:** Build a post-purchase retention sequence targeting 
electronics buyers. Even a basic personalised recommendation email 
within 24 hours of purchase would land in the highest-leverage window.

---

## Finding 7 — Smartphones are hiding poor performance across electronics
**Metric:** Smartphones 3.63% on 127k views. Cameras 0.36%, clocks 1.46%  
**So what:** Electronics' strong headline number masks two underperforming 
sub-categories. Cameras have effectively zero funnel activity — the 
product page is not working. Clocks drive meaningful traffic but convert 
at less than half the smartphone rate.  
**Recommendation:** Audit camera and clocks product pages immediately. 
These are quick wins if the issue is fixable page experience.

---

## Cohort retention summary
**Metric:** Day-1 retention 6.75%, day-7 retention 2.5%, day-14 retention 1.6%  
**So what:** The steepest drop is day 1 to day 2 (6.75% to 4.24%), 
meaning the 24-hour window after first purchase is the highest-leverage 
retention moment in the entire customer lifecycle.  
**Recommendation:** Any retention intervention should fire within 24 hours 
of first purchase, not after 7 days when most of the opportunity is gone.

---

## Cart abandonment summary
**Metric:** 38.3% overall abandonment. Auto accessories alarm 78.2%, 
kitchen washer 69.2%, telephone 62.8%  
**So what:** High-consideration categories (alarms, appliances) abandon 
at nearly double the rate of smartphones. Users are using the cart as 
a shortlist, not a committed purchase step.  
**Recommendation:** Introduce a save-for-later feature for high-abandonment 
categories. For mid-range Samsung and Xiaomi smartphones (the most 
abandoned individual products), test a price-match guarantee trigger 
at the cart stage.
